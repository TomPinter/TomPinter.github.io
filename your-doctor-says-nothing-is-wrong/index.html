Your Doctor Says Nothing Is Wrong.
Here's What They're Missing.

I'm Tom Pinter. I've been illness-free for 50 years. But I wasn't born healthy.

As a child, I was sick constantly — colds, infections, recurring illness. Every doctor my mother took me to said the same thing: nothing is seriously wrong. Eventually, they recommended removing my tonsils. My mother refused. She started looking for answers that doctors weren't looking for. What she found changed everything.

And it is directly relevant to you if your doctor says your labs are normal, but you feel terrible.

This article covers the same 7 causes explained in the video below — but goes significantly deeper on each one: what the signs are, what I do about them specifically, and why single-intervention approaches almost never work.

Why "Normal Labs" Don't Mean You're Healthy

Modern blood tests are designed to detect disease. They are not designed to measure the difference between sick and optimal.

The reference ranges on your lab results — the numbers that define normal — are calculated from the average values of people who are already using the healthcare system. People who are not well. When your doctor says your labs are normal, what they are actually saying is: you are not detectably diseased by our current measures. That is not the same as healthy.

Medical training focuses on diagnosing and treating conditions with drugs or procedures. Nutritional causes, environmental causes, structural causes — these receive very little attention in medical school. That is not a criticism of individual doctors. It is a description of how the system was built.

I have no medical degree. What I have is 50 years of evidence in my own body and decades of conversations with thousands of people who were told the same thing I was told as a child. I am not the authority. I am the result.

The 7 Causes — In Detail

There are seven causes I have found consistently behind chronic symptoms that blood tests miss. None of them operate in isolation — they interact. Here is what they are.

Cause 1

Chronic Nutritional Deficiency

The most fundamental cause. You can eat 2,000 calories a day and still be severely deficient in the specific micronutrients your body needs to function.

The human body requires what nutritional scientists call the 90 essential nutrients: 60 minerals, 16 vitamins, 12 essential amino acids, and 3 essential fatty acids. They are called essential because your body cannot manufacture them — each must come from food or supplementation every day. Each is required to prevent one or more specific health conditions.

The problem is the soil. For over a hundred years, industrial agriculture has used NPK fertilizer — nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium — because it maximizes crop yield. It does not replace the other 57 minerals the body needs. A US Senate document from 1936 already confirmed that American farmland had been depleted of its mineral content. That was 90 years ago.

Joel Wallach, a naturopathic physician and research scientist who conducted over 17,500 autopsies on animals and humans, spent decades connecting specific mineral deficiencies to specific chronic diseases. His conclusion: nearly every chronic degenerative condition he examined traced to a preventable nutritional deficiency.

My mother discovered this when I was a child. She put me on vitamin supplements and shifted us to nutrient-dense food. The recurrent infections stopped. I have been taking food supplements for over 50 years.

In theory, eating very high-quality food could provide sufficient vitamins and minerals, but due to environmental pollution, we now need even more of these nutrients to neutralize harmful effects.

How to recognize it

Frequent illness that keeps returning despite rest. Slow recovery from minor infections. These were my symptoms as a child — the pattern that drove my mother to start looking for answers the doctors weren't looking for. You may also experience persistent fatigue that doesn't improve with sleep.

What I do

My mother started me on vitamin supplements as a child. The recurring infections stopped. I have maintained daily supplementation for over 50 years.

My core daily combination for the last 20 years:

  • 1,000 mg vitamin C (can be split across meals)
  • B-50 complex + 250 mg additional B1
  • 10,000 IU vitamin A
  • 400–4,000 IU vitamin D
  • 400 IU vitamin E
  • Mineral complex including 500 mg calcium, 250 mg magnesium, zinc, iron etc.
  • Kelp trace element tablet

I take these with my first substantial meal. The brand rarely matters — cheaper supplements from reputable sources work just as well in my experience. If you have a larger body or high physical exertion, after a few weeks you can increase to 2–3 times this combination per day. B vitamins will make urine appear more yellow — that is normal and expected.

One important caution: these do not work like medications where one compound produces one effect. Think of them as gears in a mechanism — each has a function, and if you take only one while neglecting others, the system does not necessarily improve. Provide the entire range together.

When someone starts taking a comprehensive supplement combination for the first time, it is common to experience temporary changes in the first few weeks — changes in energy, digestion, or sleep. This is not a side effect. It is the body beginning to repair. Stored toxins that were previously stuck in tissues get released as the body finally has the nutrients to process them. Some people sleep more during this period. Drink more water, allow more rest. As I say: better out than in.

Cause 2

Chronic Dehydration

Not dramatic thirst — the subtle, sustained kind most people never recognize as dehydration. The body's thirst signal is unreliable. Afternoon fatigue, mental fog, a hunger that doesn't quite resolve with food — these are frequently signs of dehydration.

The advice to drink eight glasses of water a day ignores individual variation. Your needs depend on climate, activity level, and what you eat. The body regulates fluid balance using salts.

A government-funded study involving 10,000 people with high blood pressure removed all salt from the diet of one group. They did not improve. The other group was encouraged to salt their food and to increase calcium intake. 85% of them resolved their high blood pressure without any medication. The problem was not salt — it was mineral imbalance.

Monitor hydration daily by urine color and adjust based on conditions, not a formula.

How to recognize it

Hunger that appears just two to three hours after a full meal. Cravings for sweets. In hot weather or after exercise: headaches, or a feeling of faintness. These are signs the body is short on water or salt — not signs that something is fundamentally wrong.

What I do

I carry a 1-liter bottle almost everywhere I go. I monitor urine color daily — pale yellow means good hydration; dark yellow means I need more water immediately. If I feel hungry two or three hours after a proper meal, I drink a large glass of water first. Often the hunger disappears. This habit alone has prevented a significant amount of unnecessary eating over the years.

First thing in the morning, I drink water before anything else — during the night, a significant amount of water is lost through breathing. During intense exercise, hot weather, sauna, or heavy physical work, I add salt to my water or food — the body sweats out sodium and needs it replaced. Regular table salt, purified sea salt, or Himalayan salt all work well.

Cause 3

Accumulated Toxin Burden

One most doctors never discuss. Environmental toxins — in water, food packaging, air, and cleaning products — don't leave the body through normal metabolism. They accumulate in fat tissue over years and decades. A Bloomberg investigation documented how synthetic chemicals introduced in the 1950s are now detectable in virtually every human blood sample tested worldwide — not trace amounts, but significant accumulation.

The body holds on to fat partly to contain these stored toxins. This is one reason people can eat well, exercise regularly, and still feel that the body is not healing.

There is a direct connection between toxin burden and body composition. If there is not enough fat available and the body has not been given the nutritional resources to safely process what is stored, it will resist releasing fat — because it needs fat for nerve and hormone function, and because releasing contaminated fat would flood the body with toxins it cannot handle. This is one reason people can eat well, exercise regularly, and still feel that the body is not healing or changing.

The detoxification protocol I use is built around niacin — vitamin B3. Niacin produces a flush response that mobilizes stored toxins from fat tissue. Combined with exercise and sweating, this allows the body to eliminate what it has been retaining. I have used this consistently since the 1990s.

How to recognize it

Persistent difficulty losing body fat despite consistent effort with diet and exercise. The feeling that the body is "stuck." Low energy that doesn't improve with nutrition or rest alone. These patterns often indicate the body is managing a toxin load it cannot clear through normal processes.

What I do

The detoxification system I use is built around niacin — vitamin B3 specifically, and not niacinamide. Niacinamide is the form found in most commercial supplements, and it does not produce the same effect. Pure niacin produces a flush response — temporary redness and warmth — that mobilizes stored toxins from fat tissue.

I first encountered this in a supervised program. After participants took niacin and exercised, they spent time in a sauna to sweat out mobilized toxins. Tests before and after the program showed significant improvements in mental performance. I have maintained this practice since the 1990s.

The protocol, step by step:

  • Start with a small dose — 25 to 50 mg (a quarter or half of a 100 mg tablet)
  • Take it alongside the full daily supplement combination — antioxidants are needed to handle the released toxins safely — after a meal directly or when you are ready to exercise
  • Exercise for at least 20 minutes, to increase circulation and drive the mobilized toxins toward the skin
  • Sweat in a sauna if possible — at least once per week ideally
  • Ensure adequate fat intake that day — the body needs new, clean fat to replace contaminated fat tissue it is willing to release. If fat intake is low, a tablespoon of MCT oil at this time helps.

Intermittent fasting is also great — eating within an eight-hour window each day, giving the body sixteen hours to regenerate. During the fasting period, water and unsweetened coffee or tea are fine. This activates autophagy, the process by which the body breaks down and replaces damaged or old cells.

Cause 4

The Dental Health Connection

Cause four surprised me even after years of research: the connection between dental health and systemic illness.

Root canal-treated teeth are dead teeth. The biological processes that keep a living tooth healthy — circulation, immune access, cellular repair — no longer function. Bacteria survive there and produce toxic byproducts that enter the bloodstream continuously, even when the surrounding tissue shows no obvious infection. Standard dental X-rays often miss this.

A documentary called Root Cause interviewed physicians, oncologists, and biological dentists who investigated this connection clinically. One physician documented that in a sample of women with breast cancer, the overwhelming majority had a root canal tooth on the same side as their tumor. That is not proof of causation — but it is a pattern that warrants serious attention.

I had a root canal tooth for years before connecting it to issues I had not been able to resolve through nutrition and supplementation alone. After consulting a dentist and having it extracted, specific symptoms cleared.

My position: as long as you have a root-canaled tooth, it is difficult to fully resolve other health problems.

How to recognize it

This cause is particularly difficult to recognize because it may not produce local symptoms. There may be no pain, no visible swelling, and nothing abnormal on a standard dental X-ray. The signs are systemic — chronic inflammation, immune system issues, or persistent health problems that don't respond to other interventions. If you have unexplained chronic symptoms and you have one or more root-canaled teeth, the connection is worth investigating.

What I do

If you have unexplained chronic symptoms and root-canaled teeth, consult a biological dentist who uses a dental CT scan (three-dimensional imaging) rather than standard X-rays. This may be the only way to see what is actually happening inside the tooth and surrounding bone. A good biological dentist also understands the systemic connections that most standard dentists are not trained to consider.

Cause 5

Insufficient Movement — The Right Kind

Cause five: insufficient movement — specifically the right kind. When I was younger, I cycled extensively. My reason was simple: I wanted to lose weight. It didn't work. When I shifted to strength training, my body composition changed. More muscle fundamentally changes how your metabolism works — the body burns more energy, and its composition shifts.

But there is a second reason this matters. The lymphatic system — which clears cellular waste and supports immune function — has no pump. It moves entirely through muscle contraction. The more functional muscle you carry, the more efficiently your body drains what doesn't belong there.

The lymphatic connection

The lymphatic system has no pump. Blood circulation is driven by the heart. Lymph moves entirely through muscle contraction. The more functional muscle you have, and the more frequently those muscles contract, the more efficiently your body clears what does not belong.

This is why sedentary people accumulate waste products more quickly. And it is why building muscle is not just an aesthetic goal but a direct intervention for immune health and detoxification.

I later discovered variable resistance training — specifically the system developed by Dr. John Jaquish, which uses special resistance bands rather than fixed weights. Conventional weights apply the same load through the entire movement. Variable resistance increases as you get stronger at each point, matching the natural resistance curve of human anatomy. Research shows this builds significantly more muscle. I recommend looking up Dr. John Jaquish's explanations online — it is difficult to convey in words alone how different the approach is.

What I do

I maintain two distinct movement practices, and neither replaces the other.

First, structured strength training — using variable resistance. This builds and maintains the muscle mass that drives lymphatic circulation and metabolic health.

Second, a minimum of 45 minutes outdoors every day — walking, gardening, chopping wood, any physical outdoor activity. This is a separate element from structured training. Low-intensity continuous movement keeps the lymphatic system active between training sessions. Ideally this happens when the sun is shining, but a walk is valuable even at night. If you do not have a garden or park nearby, walking around the block serves the same function.

Cause 6

Poor Sleep Quality

Cause six is poor sleep — not just insufficient hours, but the wrong quality of sleep at the wrong time. The body performs most of its repair and regeneration during deep sleep. No supplement protocol or amount of exercise can compensate for a chronic deficit in this window.

Two things consistently disrupt sleep quality. First: screens after dark. Melatonin production is triggered by darkness and suppressed by blue light. This mechanism cannot be bypassed without consequences. Second: sleep timing. The recovery that happens before midnight is different and more restorative than what happens in the early morning hours.

One factor most people never investigate: the sleeping environment itself. Geopathic stress from underground water sources and electromagnetic fields from bedroom wiring can chronically disrupt sleep quality independent of habits. If you have slept poorly in the same location for years, consider where you are sleeping, not only how.

If sleep doesn't improve with these adjustments, the cause is often mental — unresolved stress running through the night.

The melatonin mechanism

Naturally, when it gets dark, we should start feeling sleepy on our own — this happens because the brain produces melatonin, a hormone that induces sleep. Melatonin production is triggered by darkness and suppressed by blue light. Staring at bright screens in the hours before sleep interferes with this mechanism directly. This is not a minor inconvenience — it is a disruption to a fundamental biological timing system. A blue-light filter on screens can help. Melatonin can also be taken as a supplement when needed.

Sleep timing

The recovery that happens before midnight is different and more restorative than what happens in the early morning hours. Getting a few hours of sleep before midnight is not an old saying — it reflects a real difference in sleep architecture. Night shifts and chronically irregular sleep schedules interfere with this in ways that cannot be fully compensated for.

The sleeping environment

In modern culture, people sleep in the same spot for years or even decades — something that was not common historically. It is worth investigating whether there are underground water veins or geopathic stress zones beneath the sleeping area. These can chronically disrupt sleep quality independent of habits.

Electrosmog from bedroom wiring is another factor most people never investigate. At a minimum, cutting power to unnecessary devices and wiring in the bedroom is a practical step. Grounding and electromagnetic neutralizing devices exist and can be measured — if this is suspected, there are professionals who can assess a bedroom.

If you have slept poorly in the same location for years, the question worth asking is not only how you sleep, but where you sleep. Changing nothing about habits but changing the bed location within the same room has resolved chronic problems for some people.

When environment is not the issue

If adjusting environment and timing does not resolve the problem, the cause is often mental. Stressful thoughts run through the night and prevent the nervous system from settling. This connects directly to Cause 7. Meditation, journaling, and physical activity during the day all help reduce the mental load that keeps sleep light. The deeper solution is building a life where there is less to worry about — which is a longer-term process, but a real one.

Cause 7

Chronic Stress and Unresolved Emotional Patterns

Cause seven connects all the others: chronic stress and unresolved emotional patterns.

Short stress vs. chronic stress

Short bursts of stress are only dangerous when extreme. The body was designed to handle acute stress — it ends when the threat passes, and the system returns to normal. The greater threat today is chronic stress: remaining in unpleasant situations for years. At work, at home, in financial conditions that create constant background tension. The body was never designed to endure stress hormones endlessly. In nature, the stress response ends. In modern life, it can persist for decades.

When the stress response is chronically activated — elevated cortisol, sustained adrenaline — the body operates in emergency mode. In an emergency, growth and repair shut down. You can follow every protocol correctly. But if you are living in chronic fear, anger, or grief, the body cannot use what you are giving it.

Research into spontaneous disease remissions — cases where serious illness resolved without conventional treatment — consistently found one common thread. These people had fundamentally changed the emotional state their body was living in.

The emotional dimension

Throughout life, people accumulate experiences that leave emotional marks. Even when these are ignored or rationalized, their energy remains, influencing mood, perception, and physical state. This is not mysticism — it is physiology. Unresolved fears, guilt, or chronic insecurity create a baseline of low-level activation that compounds over years.

What I do

My daily protocol includes a minimum of 15 minutes of meditation to eliminate thought patterns that would keep the body in a stress response. Modern life is structured to keep that response activated. Changing it requires the same attention as diet, sleep, or supplementation.

The practice is simple: sit with a straight back, close your eyes, and observe your thoughts as they arise. Let them pass without engaging them, until there is nothing left. Relaxing music or ambient sound can help. Many guided meditations are available on YouTube and various apps if you prefer structure.

The immediate purpose of meditation is to dissolve the thought patterns that would otherwise keep the body in a stress response through the night and into the next day. The longer-term purpose is developing the capacity to face challenges with composure — changing your reaction to events by changing the meaning you give to them.

They interact. That is why single-intervention approaches rarely produce the result people are looking for.
